# 🎯 Problem Identification (LU 2)

## 1. The Real Problem Statement
**Kalvium students** lose access to validated academic knowledge and critical placement mentorship because this information currently lives in fragmented, informal channels (WhatsApp/Discord) that disappear or become unsearchable over time, making every junior student repeat the same academic mistakes their seniors already solved.

## 2. The Rejected Weak Version
> "Students need a digital platform to share notes, placement experiences, and hackathon details."

### ❌ Why this was REJECTED:
This is a **solution disguised as a problem**. It focuses on what we want to build (the "platform") rather than the underlying pain. It doesn't explain *why* the current behavior is failing or what the actual loss is to the student (career growth and academic efficiency).

## 3. Root Cause Analysis
Why does this problem keep existing?
1. **Transience**: WhatsApp groups have a "scrolling limit" on memory; once a chat moves up, the knowledge is effectively deleted.
2. **Noise-to-Signal Ratio**: High-value interview tips are buried under thousands of daily memes and informal peer chats.
3. **Institutional Silos**: Students in different university partners (MGM, LPU, etc.) have no way to cross-reference academic resources, leading to a "luck-based" academic experience.
